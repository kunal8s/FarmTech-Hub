Pro Tips for Assessing Food Quality

1 Visual Inspection

Look for vibrant colors, uniform size (for produce), and absence of bruises or blemishes. Meat should have consistent coloring without gray areas.

2 Smell Test

Fresh food should smell pleasant and characteristic of its type. Sour, musty, or "off" odors often indicate spoilage.

3 Texture Check

Produce should feel firm but not hard. Meat should spring back when pressed. Avoid slimy or sticky surfaces.

4 Packaging Integrity

Check for intact seals, no punctures or leaks, and proper vacuum sealing where applicable. Bulging cans are a red flag.
