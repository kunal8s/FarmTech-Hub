Fresh Produce Guide

Expert tips for selecting, storing, and preparing fresh fruits and vegetables to maintain peak quality and flavor.

How to Select Perfect Produce

Summer

Selecting Fruits

  • Look for vibrant color and firm texture
  • Smell for sweet, fragrant aromas
  • Avoid bruises, soft spots, or mold
  • Heavier fruits often indicate more juice
Year-Round

Selecting Vegetables

  • Choose crisp, brightly colored items
  • Leafy greens should be vibrant, not wilted
  • Root vegetables should feel heavy for their size
  • Avoid vegetables with cuts or blemishes
Spring/Summer

Selecting Herbs

  • Look for bright green, perky leaves
  • Strong aroma indicates freshness
  • Avoid yellowing or black spots
  • Stems should be firm, not limp

Proper Storage Methods

Refrigerator

Best for most vegetables and delicate fruits. Store in crisper drawers with proper humidity.

Examples:

Leafy greens, berries, broccoli, carrots

Counter

Ideal for fruits that continue to ripen after picking. Keep in cool, dark place.

Examples:

Tomatoes, bananas, avocados, citrus

Root Cellar

Cool, humid environment perfect for root vegetables and winter squash.

Examples:

Potatoes, onions, garlic, winter squash

Freezer

Great for preserving seasonal produce. Blanch vegetables before freezing.

Examples:

Berries, corn, peas, chopped herbs in oil

Preparation Tips

Proper preparation preserves nutrients and enhances flavor. Follow these techniques for best results.

Washing

Rinse under cool running water just before use. For leafy greens, soak in cold water to remove dirt, then spin dry.

Cutting

Use sharp knives to minimize cell damage. Cut vegetables uniformly for even cooking.

Peeling

Leave skins on when possible (washed well) as they contain valuable nutrients and fiber.

Ethylene Gas Guide

Some fruits produce ethylene gas which can accelerate ripening (and spoilage) of nearby produce. Keep these groups separate for optimal storage.
